The Premise

Marketing fails at the moments
that matter most.

Not because teams aren't talented. Not because the tactics are wrong. Because the diagnosis was wrong before anyone picked up a tactic.

Consumer behavior at threshold moments — the inflection points where identity shifts, high-stakes decisions get made, and brand relationships either deepen or collapse — follows patterns most campaign frameworks don't account for.

Good Assumptions exists to close that gap. Behavioral science made legible for brand practitioners. The thinking that happens before the brief.

"The consumer isn't confused about your message. They're in the middle of reconstructing who they are. Those aren't the same problem."

Every consumer interaction exists somewhere on a behavioral continuum. Threshold moments are where the continuum breaks — where prior patterns lose predictive power and the underlying psychology becomes the only reliable map.

That's the work here. Understanding why behavior changes when it changes, and what that means for the brands trying to show up at exactly that moment.
