# Section 2 — The Framework

**Eyebrow:** The Framework

**Title:** Marketing problems live somewhere specific. We help you find where.

**Lead:** Four moves. Each one answers a different question about a marketing problem. Walked in order, they tell you what you're actually looking at — before you decide what to do about it.

**Cards** *(GA editorial palette: teal/gold/clay/parchment)*

01 — **The Four Layers** — Where the problem lives. Strategy, Application, Execution, Operations — the four layers a marketing problem can sit in, and the reason most fixes are aimed at the wrong one.

02 — **Threshold Moments** — When the problem activates. The doorways customers stand in — the discrete decision points where attention, trust, and choice are actually made.

03 — **Biases** — Why people decide what they decide. The cognitive shortcuts that govern most marketing-relevant decisions, before reason gets involved.

04 — **Choice Vectors** — What's pulling the moment. The ambient forces — context, identity, social proof, friction — that bend a decision before it's made.
