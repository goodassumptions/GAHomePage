01 Foundation

Brand Architecture

Two brands, one shared language. The split is conceptual — GA is the editorial voice, SAEO is the intelligence substrate. Neither exists fully without the other.

Good Assumptions
Role: Publishing, education, credibility.
Tone: Earned skepticism. Calm authority. The practitioner's colleague.
Surface: Dark editorial UI + warm parchment for print.
Audience: Marketers who want to understand the psychology behind their work.
SAEO.ai
Role: Intelligence platform, diagnostic engine, chat tool.
Tone: Precise, structured, direct. The system talking.
Surface: Dark UI, same token layer as GA.
Audience: Marketers who need to locate and solve a specific problem.
The thin line: GA is the person who built the system explaining why it works. SAEO is the system itself working. A user might read GA to understand threshold moments — and then open SAEO to diagnose one. That journey should feel continuous, not like switching tools.
What they share
Token layer
Same CSS variables — bg surfaces, text tiers, entity colors, type stack.
Entity language
Amber/purple/blue/green means the same thing on both properties.
Vocabulary
Liminal transition, threshold moment, causal legibility — shared terms.
Type stack
DM Sans · DM Serif Display · DM Mono across both.
Dark ground
Warm near-black (#0f0f0e) is canonical digital treatment for both.
Component logic
Badges, cards, IDs, reframe blocks — same patterns, same rules.
What's distinct
GA-specific
— Warm parchment editorial palette (#f7f4ee) for print/white papers
— GA teal primary (#275e63) for light mode CTA
— GA gold accent (#b98f5a) for editorial highlight
— Long-form editorial layout patterns
— DM Serif Display used more liberally
SAEO-specific
— SAEO mark colors as brand tokens (--saeo-coral, --saeo-teal)
— Chat interface patterns and message treatments
— Diagnostic layer color system (S/A/E/O)
— Reasoning mode indicators in causal maps
— Frame classification badges (Diagnostic / Intervention / etc.)

08 Language

Vocabulary

The language library. Terms used across GA and SAEO with precise definitions. Use them accurately or not at all.

Core Framework Terms
Liminal Transition
noun · entity type
A discrete psychological state a consumer moves through when making a significant behavioral change. Not a funnel stage — a threshold moment with pre-state, trigger, and post-state. Always "liminal transition" — never "stage" or "step."
Threshold Moment
noun · concept
The precise point of behavioral inflection where the consumer's reasoning mode shifts. Where brands win or permanently lose standing. Interchangeable with "transition point" but "threshold" carries more editorial weight.
Collision Intelligence
noun · methodology
Breakthrough insights from the systematic combination of behavioral concepts across unrelated domains. The core methodology behind SAEO. "Ideas having sex" — not retrieval, combinatorial. Never "AI-generated" as synonym.
Causal Legibility
noun · concept
The degree to which a brand presents itself as the most causally convincing explanation for a consumer's uncertainty. At threshold moments, brands must become the most causally legible option to win both the human's inference and the AI's inference simultaneously.
Brand Standing
noun · concept
A brand's earned right to speak to a consumer's decision at a specific threshold moment. Domain-specific and earned, not assumed. Never "brand equity" when standing is the more precise term.
Problem Localization
noun · function
The SAEO diagnostic function — identifying which S/A/E/O layer a marketing failure lives in. SAEO is a problem localization engine first. Lead with this, not "intelligence platform."
Post-Resolution Fragility
noun · state
The psychological vulnerability persisting after a consumer resolves a liminal transition. Seven named states (LIM-134–LIM-140): Hollow Compliance, Consolidation Abandonment, Advocate Betrayal, Second Failure Collapse, Suppressed Grievance, Trust Overextension, Hollow Retention.
The Weight of Why
phrase · brand concept
The prior question neither production nor distribution answers: what does the consumer need to move at this threshold, and does this brand have standing to provide it. Use as a section header or editorial frame, not marketing copy.
GEO
acronym · strategy
Generative Engine Optimization. Structuring content so AI systems surface it accurately in response to consumer queries. GA's content strategy is GEO-first. Never conflate with SEO — the underlying query behavior is fundamentally different (abductive vs. deductive).
Words We Don't Use
Journey
→ transition or threshold
Funnel stage
→ transition or behavioral state
Delight / connect
→ name the specific mechanism
AI-generated
→ collision intelligence
Empower / enable
→ state what actually changes
Best practices
→ evidence-grounded / cite research
09 Language

Voice & Tone

Two voices, one epistemic foundation. GA is the author explaining the system. SAEO is the system speaking.

Good Assumptions — Editorial Voice
PreciseNames the mechanism. Doesn't hedge when evidence says otherwise.
SkepticalGenX epistemic baseline. Show your work. Acknowledge limits.
CalmNever urgent. Talking with — not at — a practitioner.
DirectClaim first, evidence second. No preamble.
ReframingChallenges received wisdom. Sets up the behavioral counter.
SAEO.ai — System Voice
DiagnosticIdentifies which layer the problem lives in before offering solutions.
Structuredlayer → transition → mechanism → tactic.
Evidence-citedNames source biases and vectors. No vague "psychology."
Non-prescriptiveSurfaces what the consumer needs, not what client wants to hear.
AbductiveFrames answers as "most likely given these signals."
✗ Don't
It's really important that marketers understand how consumer psychology plays a role in the exciting journey through the funnel.
✓ Do
Funnel stages don't exist as psychological states. Consumers transition between discrete behavioral modes — and the transition itself is where brands lose.
✗ Don't
Understanding cognitive biases can help you better connect with customers on their journey.
✓ Do
It's not about understanding your audience better. It's about understanding what your audience's nervous system is optimized to do — and whether your brand is working with it or against it.
